### Overview
Apple conducts business ethically, honestly, and in full compliance with applicable laws and regulations. The principles of business conduct are honesty, respect, confidentiality, and compliance. If you know of a possible violation, you must notify your manager, HR, Legal, Internal Audit, Finance, or the Business Conduct office.

### Conflicts of Interest
A conflict of interest is any activity that is inconsistent with or opposed to Apple's best interest, or that gives the appearance of impropriety or divided loyalty. Do not conduct Apple business with family members or others with whom you have a significant personal relationship unless approved in writing.

### Outside Employment and Inventions
Employees must notify their manager before taking any other employment. Do not use any time at work or any Apple assets for another job, outside business, or invention. Do not use confidential Apple information to benefit another employer or outside business.

### Personal Investments and Buying Stock
Never buy or sell stock when aware of material, nonpublic information. This applies to Apple stock and third-party stock. Do not give others tips based on material nonpublic information. Employees are prohibited from investing in derivatives of Apple stock.

### Harassment and Discrimination
Apple is committed to providing a workplace free of discrimination and sexual harassment. Harassment can be verbal, visual, or physical. Report incidents to Human Resources, a supervisor or manager, or the Business Conduct Helpline.

### Confidential Employee Information
Information about employment history, compensation, health, or disciplinary matters is confidential and should be shared only with those who have a business need to know. Do not share outside Apple unless there is a legal or business reason and manager approval.

### Workplace Privacy
Subject to local law and Apple's review process, Apple may access, search, monitor, and archive data and messages sent, accessed, viewed, or stored on devices used to conduct Apple business. Employees should not expect personal information on Apple systems to be private.

### Public Speaking and Press Inquiries
All public speaking engagements relating to Apple's business or products must be pre-approved by your manager and Corporate Communications. All inquiries from the press or the financial analyst community must be referred to Corporate Communications or Investor Relations.

### Protecting Apple Assets and Information
Protect Apple's property and information. Do not let Apple secrets fall into the wrong hands. Keep track of Apple assets and information entrusted to you, and prevent loss, misuse, waste, or theft. Use Apple assets only for legal and ethical purposes.

### Confidential Apple Information
Never disclose confidential, operational, financial, trade secret, or other business information without verifying with your manager that disclosure is appropriate. Shared confidential information should be limited to a need-to-know basis and protected by an NDA when shared with third parties.

### Apple Identity and Trademarks
Before publicly using the Apple name, product names, service names, taglines, or the Apple logo, review Apple's Trademark List and Corporate Identity Guidelines. Questions about use of Apple marks should be directed to Apple Corporate Identity.

### Accuracy of Records and Reports
Records and reports must be full, fair, accurate, timely, and understandable. Never misstate facts, omit critical information, or modify records or reports to mislead others.

### Money Laundering
Money laundering is strictly prohibited. Warning signs include attempts to make large cash payments, payments from unusual accounts, and transactions forming unusual patterns such as bulk purchases of gift cards or many repetitive cash payments.

### Records and Information Management and Legal Hold
Apple may place a legal hold on records and information to prevent destruction, alteration, deletion, or modification. Recipients of a legal hold must follow the instructions provided by Legal until written notice removes the hold.

### Customer and Third-Party Information
Customers, suppliers, and others disclose confidential information to Apple for business purposes. Protect and maintain the confidentiality of this information. Failure to protect customer and third-party information may result in legal liability.

### Non-Disclosure and Confidentiality Agreements
Never share confidential information outside Apple unless a non-disclosure or confidentiality agreement is in place. Original copies of such agreements must be forwarded to the legal department.

### Third-Party Intellectual Property
Apple's policy is not to knowingly use the intellectual property of any third party without permission or legal right. If you suspect Apple may be infringing a third-party intellectual property right, contact the legal department.

### Giving and Receiving Business Gifts
Employees may not give or receive gifts or entertainment to or from current or potential vendors, suppliers, customers, or other business associates unless all policy conditions are met. Cash is never an acceptable gift. Gifts to government officials require advance review.

### Competition and Trade Practices
Do not agree with competitors or exchange information with competitors on price, policies, contract terms, costs, inventories, marketing plans, capacity plans, or other competitively significant terms. Always consult the Competition Law Team when you have a question.

### Open Source Software
Before using, modifying, or distributing open source software for Apple infrastructure or as part of an Apple product or service development effort, review Apple's Open Source Software Policy and contact Legal for approval.

### Governments as Customers
Governments often place special bidding, pricing, disclosure, and certification requirements on firms with which they do business. Discuss these requirements with Government Affairs or your local Apple Legal representative before bidding for government business.

### No Bribery or Corruption
Apple does not offer or accept bribes or kickbacks in any form and does not tolerate corruption in connection with any business dealings. This applies to both government officials and private parties.

### Business Conduct Helpline
The Business Conduct Helpline is available 24/7 to all employees worldwide to help answer questions on business conduct issues, policies, regulations, and compliance with legal requirements. It also allows employees to advise Apple of situations that may require investigation or management attention.
